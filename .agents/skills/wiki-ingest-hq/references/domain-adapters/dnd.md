# DND adapter for wiki-ingest-hq

Use this reference whenever the source is DND or similar tabletop module content.

## DND-specific expectations

### 1. Chapters are not enough
Do not stop at chapter summaries. Extract reusable knowledge into standalone pages for important:
- NPCs
- locations
- items
- monsters
- factions
- events

### 2. Important scenes need operational detail
For important scenes, preserve enough detail to answer:
- how the scene begins
- what gates entry or progress
- what the important nodes or sub-areas are
- what information, danger, reward, or transformation happens there
- what consequences or follow-up hooks emerge

### 3. Locations must be usable, not atmospheric only
A good location page should explain not just mood, but what happens there, what can be learned there, and why it matters.

### 4. Event pages must explain process, not just purpose
Do not write only what the event advances. Write what actually happens, including steps, thresholds, turning points, and outcomes where source-backed.

### 5. Item pages need function and meaning
Do not only record where an item appears. Include what it is, what it does or implies, how it is obtained, and why it matters.

### 6. NPC pages must stand alone when they are reusable
If an NPC can recur, deliver information, embody a faction, or shape player decisions, give them a standalone page.

### 7. DM-style queryability is the quality bar
The ingest should support questions about appearance, effects, triggers, consequences, relationships, progression, and scene structure.
